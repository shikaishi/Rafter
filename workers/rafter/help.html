Rafter Help

How to use the Quote Builder + settings
Back to Rafter

You build quotes on a phone or tablet. Face ID or fingerprint signs you in. Photos come from your gallery. Submit when you're done — Rafter sends the PDF to ServiceM8 and attaches it to a new job.

Signing in

First time? Your boss sends you an email. Tap the link, set up Face ID (or fingerprint), and you're in. No password to remember.

Next time you open Rafter on the same device, it knows you instantly — your phone asks for your face or your finger, and you're straight into the form.

iCloud users: if you've set up Face ID on your iPhone, your iPad gets it automatically. Same Apple account, same passkey. No second invite needed.
Switching iPhone to Android (or vice versa)? Your Face ID doesn't carry across. Ask your boss for a fresh invite on the new device.

Building a quote

  1. Open Rafter on your phone or tablet. Sign in with Face ID.
  2. Fill in the customer details at the top — name, address, contact.
  3. Tap a section (Excavation, Construction, whatever your business uses).
  4. Add items from your price book. Type custom items if you need to.
  5. Add photos by tapping the picker — they're already sorted by section.
  6. Hit Preview PDF to check it looks right.
  7. Hit Submit to send it to ServiceM8.

About five minutes from open to sent, once you know the form.

Adding photos

Tap the section you want photos in. Tap the photo picker — your camera roll opens. Pick one or more. They upload while you keep working on the rest of the quote.

Photos get compressed automatically so they don't bog down the PDF or the customer's email. You don't need to do anything.

If a photo fails to upload (slow signal, file too big), you'll see a red message next to it. Try again — usually works second time.

Previewing the PDF

The Preview PDF button opens a new tab with your quote PDF. Nothing gets sent. Safe to check.

If the preview looks wrong (typo, missing item, wrong photo), close the preview tab and edit the form. Preview again until it's right.

Sending the quote

When the preview looks good, hit Submit. Rafter:

  • Sends the PDF to ServiceM8
  • Creates a job on it
  • Attaches the PDF to the job

You'll see a confirmation with the quote reference — something like Q-20260613-0945 (the date and time it was sent).

If submit fails once, try again. If it fails twice in a row, raise a support request below — there's something on our side we should look at.

Changing a quote you already sent

Customer rings, wants something added or changed? You don't start over.

  1. Open Rafter
  2. Tap Find quote up the top
  3. Find the quote in the list — recent ones are at the top
  4. Tap it. Every section, item, quantity, and photo is exactly how you left it.
  5. Make your change
  6. Hit Submit — same ServiceM8 job, new PDF, customer can be sent the updated version

If the ServiceM8 job has been deleted in the meantime, Rafter tells you straight up instead of failing silently.

What if something doesn't work

"Operator not found" on the home page
Check the URL in your address bar matches what your boss sent. If the URL is right and you still see this, raise a support request below.
Face ID isn't working
Refresh the page first. If still not working, ask your boss to send you a fresh invite — that re-enrols Face ID on this device.
"You need a Rafter invitation from your admin" message
Your device hasn't been enrolled yet, or your invite hasn't gone through. Ask your boss to send you an invite to your work email.
A photo won't upload
Usually slow Wi-Fi. Move to better signal and tap upload again. If it still fails after a few tries, the photo might be too big — pick a smaller one.
Submit failed
Try once more. Two failures in a row means raise a support request below — there's something on our side to check.
"This account doesn't have access to this operator"
You're signed into the wrong business. Tap "Sign out & try a different sign-in" on the error screen and pick the right one.

You set up Rafter for your business and manage your team. Most settings live behind the cog at the top of the Quote Builder — that's your control panel.

Connecting ServiceM8 the first time

You did this during setup. If you need to reconnect (token expired, swapped accounts, something broke), open Settings, scroll to Connect ServiceM8, and click reconnect.

Rafter only reads from ServiceM8 (templates, materials, staff) and writes new jobs when you submit a quote. Your existing jobs and customers in ServiceM8 are not touched.

Inviting your team

Settings → Team Access.

  1. Pick a staff member from the dropdown (it's pulled live from your ServiceM8 staff list)
  2. Their email auto-fills
  3. Click Send invitation
  4. They get an email from Rafter with a link
  5. They tap the link, set up Face ID, they're in. About thirty seconds.

You can only invite people who are in your ServiceM8 staff list AND have an email on file there. This is on purpose — stops accidental invites to customers, accountants, or whoever else might've ended up in your address book.

If a staff member is missing from the dropdown, add them in ServiceM8 first (with an email), then hit the refresh button at the top of Team Access to re-pull.
If the invite email doesn't arrive, Rafter gives you a copyable link so you can text it instead. Belt and braces.

Removing a team member

Settings → Team Access → trash icon next to their row → confirm.

This removes them from this business only. If they work for another business that also uses Rafter, their account there is unaffected.

Two safety rules:

  • You can't remove yourself.
  • You can't remove the only admin.

Refreshing the team list

Top-right of the Team Access pane. If a team member just accepted their invite and you want to confirm, hit refresh — the row should move from Pending Invitations to Active Members.

Setting up sections and photos

Settings → Sections & Photos.

Where sections come from

Sections come from your ServiceM8 job templates. To add a new section, rename one, or remove one, do it in ServiceM8 first — then in Rafter hit Sync with ServiceM8 to mirror it across.

Rafter has a safety check: if ServiceM8 returns zero templates (token blip, network issue), Rafter refuses to wipe your sections. Try the sync again in a minute.

Ordering sections

Drag sections to reorder. Whatever order you set is the order on the quote PDF AND the order in the operator's photo picker. One lever.

Managing photos in a section

Tap a section to expand it.

  • Add: drag images into the section grid
  • Delete: tap delete on the photo
  • Move: send a photo to a different section
  • Copy: duplicate a photo into another section

Photos compress automatically. You don't need to worry about file size or dimensions.

Branding (colours, logo, T&Cs)

Settings → Business Configuration → Branding.

Eight colour palettes available. Pick one, hit save, your PDFs re-render in that palette instantly. Easy to try a few and pick what fits your business.

Logo: drag a PNG, JPG, or SVG up to 2MB. It shows on every PDF you send.

Bank details, payment, T&Cs

Settings → Business Configuration:

  • Bank Details: account name, BSB, account number — shows on every PDF
  • Payment Thresholds: how you bill across milestones for different job-value bands
  • Terms & Conditions: list them in order — appear on every PDF
  • Credentials: list your trade licences and insurance — appear on the quote
  • Email Template: edit the email body customers receive with their quote

What if something doesn't work

Settings page won't load — bounces to a sign-in screen
Your session expired and the settings page can't use your Face ID. Workaround: open the Quote Builder URL first (Face ID works there), then click the cog to come back to Settings. We're working on a proper fix.
Team Access shows "Couldn't load team data"
Refresh the page once. If it still fails, raise a support request below — there's a backend integration that might need looking at.
"Sync with ServiceM8" says "0 templates" or refuses
Rafter refuses to wipe your sections if ServiceM8 returns empty (it's a safety check against token blips). Try sync again in a minute. If it keeps happening, your SM8 connection may have expired — reconnect from Settings.
Team member can't accept their invite
First, check the invite hasn't expired (revoke and re-send if needed). If the link doesn't work in their browser, copy the manual link from the invite confirmation screen and text it to them.
Reading a member's role as "Member" when they're actually an Admin
Known cosmetic issue — doesn't affect their actual access. They have full admin powers regardless of the badge text. We're working on a fix.
